Comparative Study on the Different types of motherboards

"Motherboards: Foundations of versatile computing architectures."
What is a motherboard?

It is a single printed circuit board that we now know as the motherboard, holds the most important components within a computer which are the CPU, RAM, control functions, Input and Output sockets, display adapters, and etc. What makes this component extremely crucial is the fact that its role is to connect to enable each component connected to it, to allow the system to utilize the components power and use.
AT motherboard

- Made by IBM in 1984


- Standard of early computers, but was bulky
ATX motherboard

- Made by Intel


- Improved AT model, became the new standard
BTX motherboard

- Made by Intel


- Better cooling, but had worse incompatibility
Extended-ATX motherboard

- Variant of the ATX motherboard


- Used for workspaces that needed high performance
LPX motherboard

- Made for small desktops


- Known for its small size, but was replaced because of poor ventilation
Micro ATX motherboard

- Smaller ATX


- Similar features with ATX, but smaller
Mini ITX motherboard

- Made by Via Technologies


- Known for its extremely compact size
Mini ATX motherboard

- Smaller ATX


- A smaller version of the Micro ATX
Pico BPX motherboard

- Compact version of the BTX


- Efficient cooling, but replaced by the Mini ITX
Standard ATX motherboard

- Flexible to optimizations


- Is the current world standard for motherboards
